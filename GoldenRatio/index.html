Using Golden Ratio in your design

During the 1800s, Mathematicians & Philosopher Adolf Zeising, found the golden ratio is expressed in the arrangement of parts such as leaves and branches along the stems of plants and of veins in leaves.

Later, to the skeletons of animals and the branchings of their veins and nerves. In these patterns in nature he saw the golden ratio operating as a universal law.

So what is a golden ratio?

The golden ratio is also called the golden mean or golden section . Other names include medial section, divine proportion, divine section , golden  proportion, and golden number.

In mathematics, two quantities are in the golden ratio if their ratio is the same as the ratio of their sum to the larger of the two quantities.

The figure shown, illustrates the geometric relationship.

By incorporating quarter-arc curve across two sides of perfect square will form a golden spiral if scaled 1.618 smaller in a multiplied sequence - This is known as the fibonacci Spiral.

The fact that the number is an irrational number, for design & creative purposes, Golden ratio number is generally agreed at 1 : 1.618.

In design principles, incorporating ratio in your visual artwork or structure is considered as design staple.

Why using golden ratio is so important?



Scientifically what we defined as "beauty" is classified by proportion according to Gary B. Meisner in the book "The golden Ratio : The Divine Beauty of mathematics".

When the size or spaces between the object of an image or a structure is seen as proportionally correct, it is regarded as "beautiful" or "visually pleasing" by the viewer.

This formula has long been adopted & facinated by many historical figures throughout the century, the usage of golden ratio can be seen in their paintings, architectural masterpiece, designs, or photo compositions & other creations.
